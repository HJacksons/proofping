# ProofPing

ProofPing is a privacy-first real-human proof platform.

## One-liner

Proof of what’s true right now — from someone who’s actually there.

## Product Promise

ProofPing helps people get quick real-world confirmation from real humans for listings, sellers, shops, addresses, local situations, public places, queues, campus facilities, documents, and claims that AI cannot physically verify.

## Not a Q&A App

ProofPing does not compete with ChatGPT. AI can answer questions, but AI cannot physically verify a place, confirm a seller exists, check a queue or printer right now, or provide human accountability.

## Core Loop

1. User creates a proof request.
2. User shares a link (or posts for nearby helpers).
3. A real person replies with text, photo, video, or local confirmation.
4. The requester reviews replies.
5. The request is marked solved, suspicious, unresolved, or closed.
6. Optional donation, unlock credits, or urgent boost.

## First Use Cases

1. Verify before you pay (marketplace / listings).
2. What’s true right now in a busy place (campus, office, mall).

Examples:

- Is this apartment listing real?
- Can someone near this shop confirm it is open?
- Is the library printer working?
- How crowded is the study hall?
- Is there wheelchair access here?
- Does this address exist?
- Can someone local confirm this place looks like the photos?

## Later Feature Lines

- **NowProof density:** unanswered nearby asks become helper opportunities.
- **SnapBounty mode:** photograph what you need done; same proof engine, photo-first create.
- **Credits wallet:** packs of unlock credits (not card micropayments per unlock).

See `docs/NOWPROOF_SPRINT_PLAN.md`.

## Safety Rules

- 18+ only.
- No stalking.
- No tracking private people.
- No doxxing.
- No harassment.
- No romantic surveillance.
- No requests involving children.
- No medical diagnosis.
- No illegal services.
- No public ratings of private individuals.

## Design Feeling

Welcoming, calm, trustworthy, global, simple, human, warm, and serious.

The product should feel like help from a kind local person, not like a noisy social network.
